Observation 2

The asset loading works like this:
- need to load a sprite
- opens DATA\sprites.zip
- extracts it to a buffer
- catches the sprite
- release the buffer
- close the zip

When new sprite is needed, it starts again.
Now opening the zip and closing seems like an overkill,
but in reality its not happening that way. 
The OS caches the zip and the next load won't use IO, and happens at memcopy speed. (png decoding is the narrowest cross-section, still fast)
Loading assets with the above method remains lightning fast, and no need to make a "single pass" loader in most cases.
